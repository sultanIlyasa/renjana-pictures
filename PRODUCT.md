# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences stacked on one surface. The **on-screen audience** is anyone evaluating Renjana Pictures — prospective clients (brands, marketing teams, agencies, creative directors) judging whether this is the production partner for their next campaign. The goal there is broad brand awareness: whoever lands should leave remembering it. The **off-screen audience** is the prospect Sultan points at this URL — because the site is also a Sarikaya Labs showpiece, every visitor is implicitly judging Sarikaya's calibre too. In a creative field both audiences judge the same way: production quality is inferred from the craft of the page they're standing on, so the page itself must be a sample reel.

## Product Purpose

This is a **single-page company-profile landing** for *Renjana Pictures*, a broadcasting & media production company — built by **Sarikaya Labs** as a frontend-only, 100% static showpiece (dummy content, no backend, no CMS). It exists so Sultan can point a prospect at one URL and say *"this is the calibre of company-profile site I build."* The artifact must read like an Apple keynote landing page: cinematic, scroll-choreographed, premium. The signature "wow" is **scroll-driven media scrubbing** — scrolling forward and backward scrubs a pinned video/image-sequence frame-by-frame, Apple-product-page style — backed by a few beautifully orchestrated GSAP/ScrollTrigger moments rather than scattered micro-animation. Success is measured in attention held and identity remembered (and the calibre it signals), not in form fills; the contact form is visual-only and fakes its success state. The name *Renjana* (Indonesian for deep yearning / passion) is the brief: the page should carry the same feeling the work does.

## Brand Personality

**Refined · understated · premium.** Quiet luxury, not loud spectacle. Confidence through restraint — the work commands the screen, the chrome gets out of the way. Generous whitespace, deliberate pacing, motion that feels directed rather than busy. The voice is assured and economical; it never has to shout because the work earns the attention. Emotionally: mood, atmosphere, a held breath — yearning made tangible.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate stock-photo agency.** No soulless business clichés, no smiling-handshake stock, no lifeless corporate-blue palettes. This is a creative house, not a vendor.
- **Generic SaaS / startup template.** No purple-gradient-on-white SaaS slop, no gradient blobs, no tiny uppercase eyebrow kickers above every section, no identical icon-heading-text feature-card grids, no hero-metric template. None of the AI-default landing-page scaffolding.
- **Cheap / amateur production vibe.** Nothing that undercuts a premium, professional production house — no clip-art energy, no template stiffness, no cut corners. The craft bar is high because the product is craft.
- Also avoid: cluttered, over-animated showreels where autoplay chaos buries the actual work.

## Design Principles

1. **The work is the hero.** Every layout decision defers to the footage, the frame, the project. Chrome is minimal; the interface is a frame around the work, not a competitor to it.
2. **Restraint is the flex.** Confidence is shown by what's left out — whitespace, silence, a single deliberate gesture. Understatement reads as premium; over-design reads as insecure.
3. **The page is a sample reel.** Visitors judge production quality by the craft in front of them. Pixel precision, timing, and polish are not garnish — they are the pitch.
4. **Mood before message.** Renjana means yearning. Lead with atmosphere and feeling; let information arrive once the visitor is already drawn in.
5. **Memorable, not loud.** Aim for one thing a visitor can't forget — a moment of motion, a frame, a pacing choice — rather than many things competing for attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, including over imagery and video (use scrims/overlays where needed). Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation — critical for a motion-forward site; reduced-motion users get crossfades or instant states, never a broken or blank experience. Keyboard-navigable, visible focus states, captions/transcripts available for video content where it carries meaning.
